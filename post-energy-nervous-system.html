Energetic healing and the nervous system — why the body holds what the mind forgets

The mind forgets. The body does not. Long after a stressful period has passed, long after you have processed an experience consciously, the nervous system continues to hold its imprint — in the tissues, in the breath, in the way you move through the world.

Emma Fletcher · Integra Studios · Adelaide

What stress actually does to the body

When the nervous system encounters a threat — real or perceived — it activates the sympathetic response. Cortisol and adrenaline flood the system. Heart rate and breath accelerate. Muscles contract. This is the survival response, and it is extraordinarily effective at keeping us safe in moments of acute danger.

The problem is that the nervous system does not always know when the danger has passed. Particularly in cases of chronic stress or unresolved trauma, the body can remain in a low-grade state of activation indefinitely — tense, vigilant, depleted — even when the conscious mind has moved on.

This is why people sometimes cannot simply think their way out of anxiety, exhaustion or emotional reactivity. The issue is not in the thinking — it is in the nervous system's stored memory of threat.

Where energetic healing comes in

Energetic healing approaches the nervous system differently from talk therapy or medication. Rather than working through cognition or chemistry, it works through the body's own electromagnetic field — the biofield — which is intimately connected to the nervous system's regulatory processes.

Reiki and Seichem, applied by a skilled practitioner, have been shown to activate the parasympathetic nervous system — the rest-and-digest response that is the physiological opposite of the stress response. In this state, the body releases stored tension, the breath deepens, and tissues begin to soften and repair.

Sound therapy accelerates this process through entrainment — the synchronisation of the nervous system with healing frequencies that guide it away from activation and into coherence.

What this looks like in practice

Clients at Integra Studios often describe a quality of release that surprises them — emotion moving through without being overwhelming, physical sensations that feel like unwinding, a sense of space opening where there was tightness.

This is not catharsis in the dramatic sense. It is the quiet, biological process of a nervous system learning — or remembering — that it is safe.

"During the session I experienced a profound release of tension and emotional weight. Afterwards, I felt grounded, peaceful and more connected to myself than I had in a long time." — Georgie, Adelaide

Energetic healing is not a replacement for psychological support when that is needed. But for many people, it offers a way into the body's held experience that other modalities cannot always reach.
