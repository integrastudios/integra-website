The science of sound healing — what actually happens in your nervous system

When you lie in a sound healing session and feel the vibration move through your body, something real is happening at a physiological level. This is not ambient relaxation — it is a precise interaction between external frequency and your body's own electromagnetic field.

Emma Fletcher · Integra Studios · Adelaide

How sound interacts with the body

Sound is vibration. And vibration does not stop at the skin — it penetrates tissue, reaches organs, and influences the electrical activity of the nervous system. When sound frequencies are introduced into the body's field through instruments like crystal singing bowls, chimes and tuning forks, a process called entrainment occurs.

Entrainment is the tendency of a system to synchronise with an external rhythm. Your brainwaves, heart rate and nervous system all naturally entrain to the dominant frequency in their environment. This is why certain music makes you feel calm, or why the hum of machinery creates tension — your body is responding, not just hearing.

In a professional sound healing session, this entrainment is deliberate. The frequencies chosen correspond to specific physiological and energetic outcomes.

What the research shows

Peer-reviewed studies have documented significant reductions in anxiety, cortisol levels, blood pressure and heart rate following sound healing sessions. Research published in the Journal of Evidence-Based Integrative Medicine found that a single sound meditation session produced measurable reductions in tension, anger, fatigue and depressed mood.

Crystal singing bowls produce frequencies in the range of 432-528 Hz — frequencies associated with nervous system regulation and cellular repair in emerging research. Tuning forks, particularly those calibrated to specific binaural frequencies, have been shown to influence brainwave states directly, moving participants from beta (alert, stressed) into alpha and theta states associated with deep restoration.

What you might experience during a session

Clients at Integra Studios describe a range of experiences during sound healing — from deep physical heaviness and warmth, to spontaneous emotional release, to vivid imagery and altered states of awareness. These are all normal responses to the nervous system shifting registers.

Some clients feel very little during the session and notice the effects in the hours and days that follow — better sleep, reduced reactivity, a quieter mind. Others experience immediate and profound shifts. Neither is more correct than the other.
