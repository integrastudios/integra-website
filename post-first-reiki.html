What to expect from your first in-person Reiki session in Adelaide

Most people arrive at their first in-person Reiki session carrying equal parts curiosity and uncertainty. That is exactly right. You do not need to understand it before you experience it — you just need to arrive. This guide is specifically for those coming in person to see Emma at Integra Studios in Adelaide.

Emma Fletcher · Integra Studios · Adelaide

Before your session

There is very little preparation required for an in-person session. Wear comfortable, loose clothing — you will remain fully clothed throughout. Avoid a heavy meal in the hour before your session. If you can, avoid rushing to get there — arriving a few minutes early and taking a breath before you come in makes a real difference to how quickly you settle.

At Integra Studios, every session begins with a gentle conversation. There is no intake form to fill out, no clinical assessment to navigate. Just an unhurried space to share what is present for you — what has been weighing on you, what you are hoping to shift, or simply that you are not sure and wanted to try.

You will not be pushed to share more than feels comfortable. The conversation is simply an opening — a way to arrive before anything begins.

During the session

You will lie fully clothed on a treatment table in a quiet, warm space. The physical environment has been carefully considered — the room, the light, the temperature — because your surroundings directly influence how deeply your nervous system can settle. A guided meditation and sound therapy using crystal bowls, chimes and tuning forks will open the session, gently shifting you into a more receptive state before the energy work begins.

The Reiki itself is channelled through light touch or near-body work in the field just above the body, depending on what feels appropriate in that moment. The physical presence of the practitioner — in the same room, breathing the same air — creates a quality of connection that is particular to in-person work. You may feel warmth, tingling, heaviness or a floating quality. You may see colours or imagery. You may fall into that place between sleeping and waking. All of these are completely normal responses.

Nothing in the session is forced or performed. Emma follows what your energy field presents — which means every session is different, and nothing is planned in advance.

After your session

Most clients leave feeling a quality of calm they find difficult to describe — lighter, quieter, more themselves. Some feel emotional in a gentle, releasing way. Some feel very little immediately and notice the effects over the following days.

Drink water. Rest if you can. Avoid overstimulation in the hours after your session — screens, noise, social demands. Your nervous system has just done significant work, and it needs time to integrate what has shifted.

This integration period — the days following a session — is often where the most profound changes occur. Dreams become more vivid. Old patterns become more visible. Clarity emerges where there was confusion.

At Integra, we suggest a minimum of three sessions to experience what Reiki is truly capable of. Energy healing works in layers — each session builds on the last. But the best place to begin is always simply: the first one.
