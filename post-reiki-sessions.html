Why no two Reiki sessions should ever feel the same

True Reiki is not a protocol. It is a living transmission — channelled fresh in every session, for every person, at every moment. If what you have experienced felt routine, you may not yet have experienced what Reiki actually is.

Emma Fletcher · Integra Studios · Adelaide

The problem with scripted Reiki

There is a version of Reiki that follows a set hand position sequence, moves methodically from head to toe, and concludes after exactly sixty minutes. It looks like Reiki. It may even feel pleasant. But it is missing the essential element that makes Reiki transformative rather than merely relaxing.

That element is channelling — the practitioner's capacity to tune into what your energy field is presenting in this moment, and respond to it with precision rather than procedure.

Real Reiki practitioners do not decide in advance what your session will look like. They arrive with presence, open their channel, and follow what emerges. The session leads itself.

What channelled Reiki actually feels like

Clients who have experienced channelled Reiki describe something markedly different from a standard treatment. There is a quality of being truly seen — not just physically, but energetically. The practitioner is responding to what is actually present, not what is scheduled to come next.

You might feel heat, tingling, or spontaneous emotional release in areas the practitioner has not yet touched. You might receive insights during the session that feel less like thoughts and more like rememberings. You might leave feeling that something fundamental has shifted — even if you cannot name exactly what.

"I went in thinking we would be focusing on one thing, but it ended up uncovering something I had been holding onto for a long time." — Esther, Adelaide
