Sound Healing · Adelaide · Instruments

Crystal bowls, chimes and tuning forks — what each instrument does and why it matters

The instruments used in a sound healing session are not interchangeable. Each one carries a different frequency, penetrates a different layer of the body, and produces a different physiological and energetic response. Understanding what each does can deepen your experience of the session.

Emma Fletcher · Integra Studios · Adelaide

Crystal singing bowls

Crystal singing bowls are made from crushed quartz crystal, heated and spun into their form. Because the human body contains a significant proportion of crystalline structures — in the bones, in the connective tissue, in the blood — quartz crystal has a particular resonance with our physiology.

When a singing bowl is struck or played, it produces a rich, sustained tone with multiple harmonic overtones. These overtones work simultaneously on different layers of the body — the physical, the emotional and the energetic. The sustained nature of the sound allows the nervous system time to truly entrain with the frequency rather than simply react to it.

Different bowl sizes correspond to different frequencies. Larger bowls produce lower tones that resonate with the physical body and root energy centres. Smaller bowls produce higher frequencies that work more subtly with the upper energetic layers.

Chimes

Chimes introduce a different quality of sound — bright, clear, and quickly decaying. Where singing bowls hold a sustained field, chimes create precise moments of vibration that can cut through energetic density and introduce clarity.

In a healing session, chimes are often used to open or close energetic work, or to shift the quality of the field at specific moments. Their sound carries a lightness that complements the deeper resonance of the bowls — creating a full spectrum of frequency within a single session.

Tuning forks

Tuning forks are the most precise sound healing instrument. Calibrated to exact frequencies, they can be applied directly to the body or used in the biofield to produce targeted therapeutic effects.

Forks calibrated to the Solfeggio frequencies — a set of ancient tones associated with specific healing properties — have been used in sound therapy for their documented effects on brainwave states and cellular function. When placed near specific points on the body, their vibration travels through tissue and into the nervous system with remarkable precision.

Binaural tuning forks — two forks of slightly different frequencies played simultaneously — produce a third frequency in the brain itself, guiding it into specific states from beta through to delta.

At Integra Studios, all three instruments are used within every Biofield Therapy session — chosen and applied in response to what the session requires in each moment. The result is a layered, full-spectrum sound experience that supports healing at every level.
